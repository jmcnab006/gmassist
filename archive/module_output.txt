[STRUCTURE:Death House]
description: >
  A four-story brick row house (including attic), standing in the village of Barovia. The house has wooden floors, swings-out windows, and ceilings ranging from 8 to 13 feet high depending on the floor.
  The first and second floors appear well-maintained and new, with clean, oiled floors and fresh furniture.
  The third floor and attic are dusty, drafty, and covered in cobwebs.
special_features:
  - House is sentient, aware of creatures within it, and aims to lure adventurers to doom.
  - Destroying the house is temporary; it repairs itself within 2d6 hours after 1d10 days.
  - Replacement of furnishings and structural repairs occur, but items removed and destroyed undead are not replaced.
  - The dungeon (basement) level is separate and cannot repair itself.
history:
  - Original owners practiced dark arts and formed a cult, attempting extraplanar summoning and ritual murders.
  - Cultists lured prey and worshipped Strahd von Zarovich but were rejected and slaughtered by him.
  - Their spirits haunt the dungeon level.
  - The house itself repeatedly rises from ruins at its own or Strahd's will.
notable_residents:
  - Rose and Thorn: Illusionary children formed from the spirits of those locked and starved in the attic centuries ago.
  - Parents (Gustav and Elisabeth Durst): Insane cult leaders responsible for the children’s death.
